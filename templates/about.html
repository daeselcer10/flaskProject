FAQ and Credits


Frequently Asked Questions about Bookshelf


Who are you?

My name is Dae. Hi!

Are you a coder?

No. I'm an elementary school teacher! I learned (some) Python and Javascript so I could make this game. I had help (see below). I know my code...isn't the most elegant. If you want to yell at me about it, click here.

Why did you make this?

I like to have fun projects. Also, I made a paper version of it for a friend, and then wondered what a web-based version would be like.

What kinds of books are in this? What types of things should I guess?

Good question! The Bookshelf library consists of 750ish books (and counting). They are novels, novellas, plays, children's books, and creative memoirs/autobiographies. There are no scientific texts or philosophical treatises. I try to make it so that there's a good mixture of classic books that everyone is familiar with and more obscure or new titles that I think it might be fun for you to check out!

Can I suggest a book?

Sure! No promises on when/if it'll show up, but I love suggestions! Send me an email!

Why don't you show the answer after 6 guesses?

Mmm, now we're getting philosophical. After your 6 clues are gone, I want you to have the opportunity to ponder the book for a bit - who knows, maybe it'll come to you in a flash! But if it doesn't...feel free to use the clues I've given you to look the book up. You know, on the internet. What? I hear you say. And I say: yes! I want Bookshelf players to get introduced to new books, and you remember new information better when you do something with it as opposed to just being given it. Also, sometimes going outside the game can also be a part of intended play.

I am skeptical of you.

That's healthy!

Is this like Wordle? Do you play Wordle?

I guess you could say that this game was inspired by Wordle, though there are some significant differences. In Bookshelf, the answer could be any length, so the answer-input and checking mechanisms are different from Wordle's. Also, in my game, you get clues. And guess who had to write all those clues? Me. It was fun though!

And yes, I do play Wordle every day. Other daily games include Worldle, Globle, Flagle, Capitle, and Framed.

Why don't you track my win streak? Why are you tracking my play streakinstead?

Well, that's a philosophical question! Unlike games like Wordle, where you're likely to have heard of every word in the data set, some of Bookshelf's answers might not be guessable for you. You may never have read or even heard of today's book - and that's ok! It's fun to discover new things! So in an effort to keep things both fun and fair, I track your days played in a row, not games won in a row. Ta da!

Gee, I sure do love this game. What can I do to show my appreciation?

Please consider sending me a tip! I am currently a doctoral student who also works full time, so I very much need the money. Thank you! If you can't tip me, then please share the game!

Why doesn't this game have X feature? Game Y has X feature! Grrr!

I would really love to add more features to Bookshelf! I'm working on it, I swear. If you have an idea for me, send me an email, or better yet, buy me a coffee. Caffeine helps me produce.
